Life

 

Egidio Costantini was born in Brindisi in Apulia on 22 April 1912. When he was 18 the family moved to Venice where Egidio obtained a radiotelegraph operator's licence. He was taken on by the Circolo Motonautico in Venice. He was promoted rapidly and bacame the director in 1939. During the war he worked for several years as a cashier at the Banca Commerciale in Venice.

In 1945, Egidio Costantini moved from Venice to Cercivento, the home village of wife Emilia Dain (married in 1937) and started a company for the dry distillation of wood to extract essential oils. After one of his furnaces had cooled, he noticed that the walls had vitrified, with a bluish-green crystallisation. After that he started to dream of making works of art in glass.

And the dream became reality when, in 1950, Centro Studi Pittori Arte del Vetro, was formed by Egidio Costantini, Fioravante Seibezzi, Armando Tonello, and others, with the intent of having permanent place of business and showroom in Murano. One year later the Centro changed its name to Fucina degli Angeli, on the suggestion of Jean Cocteau. From that point on it was just one exhibition after the other: Basel, Paris, New York.

Egidio Costantini did not meet all these artists by chance. He chose them. He considered that affinities were important for collaboration to be fruitful, for the master glassmakers of the Fucina degli Angeli to be able to give a third dimension to the artist's design. Every real work is meditation, communication and new creation.
