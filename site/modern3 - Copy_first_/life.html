Fucina degli Angeli

Fondata da Egidio Costantini

tel: +39 347 8599026
email: info@fucinadegiangeli.com


Egidio Costantini was born in Brindisi in Apulia on 22 April 1912. After the death of his father, a navy captain, the family moved to Venice where Egidio obtained a radiotelegraph operator's licence when he was 18. However, he could not hope to be given responsibilities on a luxury liner at his age and he was taken on by the Circolo Motonautico in Venice. He was promoted rapidly and bacame the director in 1939. But the war broke out and he worked for several years as a cashier at the Banca Commerciale in Venice.

In 1945, he moved from Venice to Cercivento, the home village of Emilia Dain, nicknamed Mamma Emi, whom he had married in 1937, and started a company for the dry distillation of wood to extract essential oils. This is where he had a revelation that opened up new horizons for him. After one of his furnaces had cooled, he noticed that the walls had vitrified, with a bluish-green crystallisation. This sight fascinated him and he started to dream of making works of art in glass.

And the dream became reality when, in 1950, Centro Studi Pittori Arte del Vetro, was formed by Egidio Costantini, Fioravante Seibezzi, Armando Tonello, and others, with the intent of having permanent place of business and showroom in Murano. One year later the Centro changed its name to Fucina degli Angeli, on the suggestion of Jean Cocteau. From that point on it was just one exhibition after the other: Basel, Paris, New York.

All Egidio Costantini's work is the reflection of his meetings with others, exceptional moments of symbiosis between the concept of one and the materialisation of the concept by the other. He was convinced that he was giving art and artist a new dimention, a fully-fledged means of expression. His strength lay in this conviction, the beginnings of what became his message towards the end of his life.
